Privacy Policy

This privacy policy was created to inform you, in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679, about the data we collect, how we use it, and your options as a visitor of this website.

We understand that privacy policies often sound technical, but we have made every effort to clearly and simply explain the most important points.

Automatic Data Storage

When you visit our website, our web server automatically stores information such as:

  • the address (URL) of the accessed page
  • browser and browser version
  • the operating system used
  • the referring URL (the previously visited page)
  • hostname and IP address of the accessing device
  • date and time

These are stored in web server log files and typically deleted automatically after two weeks. We do not share this data but cannot rule out that it may be viewed in cases of unlawful behavior.

Cookies

Our website uses HTTP cookies to store user-specific data. Cookies are small text files created by your browser and stored on your device.

Cookies help make our website more user-friendly. Some cookies remain stored on your device until you delete them. They allow us to recognize your browser on your next visit.

How to Disable Cookies

You can configure your browser to notify you before cookies are set and only allow cookies on a case-by-case basis.

Disabling cookies may limit the functionality of our website.

Storage of Personal Data

Personal data that you submit to us electronically on this website (e.g., name, email address) will only be used for the specified purpose, securely stored, and not shared with third parties.

Rights According to the General Data Protection Regulation

According to the GDPR and Austrian data protection law (DSG), you have the following rights:

  • Right to rectification (Article 16 GDPR)
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") (Article 17 GDPR)
  • Right to restriction of processing (Article 18 GDPR)
  • Right to notification (Article 19 GDPR)
  • Right to data portability (Article 20 GDPR)
  • Right to object (Article 21 GDPR)

If you believe your data is being processed in violation of data protection laws, you can file a complaint with the supervisory authority: www.dsb.gv.at

TLS Encryption with https

We use https to securely transmit data on the internet. TLS (Transport Layer Security) protects confidential data during transmission.
